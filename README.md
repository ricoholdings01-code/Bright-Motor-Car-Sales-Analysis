# Bright-Motor-Car-Sales-Analysis
### Project Overview
This project mainly focus is to provide actionable business insights for the new Head of Sales at Bright Motors to support dealership expansion and inventory optimization. As a Junior Data Analyst, the goal is to transform raw transactional data into a strategic roadmap for growth.

### **1. Problem Statement**

Leadership Transition- Bright Motors has recently appointed a new Head of Sales who requires a data-driven roadmap to expand the dealership network and improve overall sales performance.
Information Gap- There is a need to transform daily transactional and pricing data into actionable insights to guide future sales and marketing strategies.
Operational Inefficiency- The company needs to identify underperforming regions and optimize inventory based on emerging customer purchasing preferences.

### **2. Project Objective**

Revenue Identification- Determine which specific car makes and models are the primary drivers of revenue for the company.
Market Analysis- Evaluate the relationship between vehicle pricing, mileage, and year of manufacture to understand market positioning.
Strategic Growth- Pinpoint high-volume sales locations and provinces to inform regional expansion plans.
Profitability Assessment- Calculate profit margins and categorize vehicle inventory into performance tiers (High, Medium, and Low Margin).

### **3. Aim of the Project**

The primary aim is to serve as a Junior Data Analyst to extract high-level insights from historical sales data.
To deliver a comprehensive suite of analytical tools—including dashboards and reports—that facilitate executive decision-making for the Head of Sales.
To provide data-backed recommendations aimed at increasing dealership profitability and operational efficiency.

### **4. Objectives (Step-by-Step Approach)**

Step 1: Planning and Architecture- Develop a data flow diagram and project roadmap to outline the ETL pipeline from raw CSV to the presentation layer.
Step 2: Data Cleaning and Processing- Load the raw dataset and perform cleaning tasks, including removing duplicates, handling missing values, and converting text-based prices into numeric formats.
Step 3: Feature Engineering- Create new metrics such as Total Revenue and Profit Margin using the following formulas:
    *$\text{Total Revenue} = \text{Selling Price} \times \text{Units Sold}$$
    *$\text{Profit Margin} = \frac{(\text{Selling Price} - \text{Cost Price})}{\text{Selling Price}} \times 100$$
Step 4: Exploratory Data Analysis- Group transactions by time periods (month, quarter, or year) and fuel types to identify seasonal trends.
Step 5: Dashboard Development- Build interactive visualizations with slicers for region, fuel type, and year to allow stakeholders to drill down into the data.
Step 6: Strategic Reporting- Summarize findings and actionable recommendations into a professional executive presentation.

### **5. Tools Used

* Miro used for brainstorming project requirements and designing the data architecture.
* Canva utilized for creating project Gantt charts and visual assets.
* Databricks employed for executing SQL queries, data cleaning, and complex transformations.
* Power BI & Data Studio used to build interactive, real-time dashboards for regional and trend analysis.
* Microsoft Excel used for in-depth data reporting, pivot tables, and initial data exploration.
* Microsoft PowerPoint used to compile the final stakeholder presentation and executive summary.
* Lovable used to deploy a live website to showcase the project findings.
